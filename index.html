Why Us?

We’re dedicated and passionate about providing the best in patient-centered care and assessment programs suitable for each child. We provide tailor-made services that meet the individual need of each and every child.

Applied Behavioral Analysis (ABA)

What is ABA Therapy? Is ABA Right for My Child?

Behavioural analysis is a branch of psychology that aims to comprehend, predict, and modify human behaviour. ABA is based on the fact that a client's behaviour is influenced by previous and current environmental experiences, or contingencies, as well as other genetic and/or environmental factors.

ABA focuses on treating the disorder's problems by facilitating the learning of suitable and functional behaviours rather than the dysfunctional or inappropriate actions. ABA is an evidence-based technique that has been shown to be useful. It helps increase language and communication skills; improve attention, focus on social skills, memory, and academics; and decrease problem behavior not only for those with Autism but also for other diseases such as ADHD, Depression, ASD, and other developmental disabilities. ABA program includes goals to address concerns provide by a team of internationally certified and fully supervised Applied Behavioral Analysis (ABA) therapists.

Speech and Language Therapy

Speech and Language Therapy is a programme that supports children and young people (0– 19) with speech, language, and communication problems. This includes problems such as:


  • Understanding a language
  • Using a language
  • Communication and interaction
  • Dysfluency (stammering)
  • Speech sounds
  • Social skills

We also help children and young people who have eating and drinking difficulties. Some of the children we help may have exceptional needs, such as a cleft palate, hearing loss, special educational needs, or disabilities. To assist in helping the child, we collaborate closely with parents, caregivers, and other experts.
Workshops, training, assessments, and group and/or individual therapy sessions are all included.

Early Intervention

When a child does not appear to be developing according to the regular timetable of new-born milestones, parents and family members are understandably concerned. At The Bridge, early intervention is a complete service that provides focused stimulation for both children and preschoolers with developmental delays, high-risk conditions, or disabilities.

The first 5 years of life are a critical time for brain development, especially if a child is delayed or restricted in development. If a child needs support to develop optimally, early intervention can help. Academic studies have demonstrated that early intervention for children with a range of different disabilities results in significant benefits to the child. The earlier the intervention, the better the outcome. The child receives individualized treatment to help empower each child to reach their full potential early who have disabilities, or who are at risk of developing them, the help they need to succeed later in life

The ultimate goal of the Early Intervention program is to assist children in learning skills related to the developmental areas that normally develop during the first three years of life such as:

  • Physical (reaching, rolling, crawling, and walking)
  • Cognitive (thinking, learning, solving problems)
  • Social (playing, feeling secure and happy)
  • Self Help (eating, dressing).

If the child has a developmental delay in one or more of these areas, the child receives individualized treatment to fulfil his or her specific requirements.

Special Educational/Transitions Program

Is your child struggling with reading, writing, mathematics, sitting still, paying attention, following instructions, or playing may be able to help ? What may appear as academic may be treatable by identifying underlying causes through comprehensive assessments conducted by our trained psychologists. evidence-based programs can effectively address these causes, helping your child achieve their goals and preparing a brighter path for their future. Bridge special education program is an inclusion bridging program, a joint collaboration with mainstream schools to support the gradual transition of children with additional needs into the mainstream classroom. It is a supervised and coordinated gradual shift into full-time inclusion.

Vocational Program

Children in our vocational programme learn critical life skills that will benefit them at home and in the community. These abilities boost self-assurance and participation in everyday activities.

The bridge’s vocational program focuses on teaching a wide range of core skills needed for employment and enhancement, such skills are:

  • Time management
  • Organisation and planning
  • Effective communication
  • Teamwork
  • Positive attitude
  • Problem solving

Occupational and Sensory Therapy

Occupational therapy is one of several services that may be provided as part of the bridge program. It benefits children with many specific conditions to improve their motor, cognitive, sensory processing, communication, and play skills.
The goal is to enhance development, minimize the potential for developmental delay, and help families to meet the special needs of their infants and toddlers.

Occupational therapy assess and treat all areas of social, emotional, behavioral, and motor skill development. They help kids and adults develop the skills to perform everyday tasks using creative methods that are always fun. Some of these areas include:

  • Sensory Processing
  • Self Care Skills
  • Classroom Accommodations
  • Dyspraxia
  • Coordination and Fine Motor Skills

Programs For Schools and Nursery

  • Screening
  • Individual Education Plan (IEP)
  • Individual Therapy Plan (ITP)
  • Educational Consulting
  • Teacher Training Workshops

Screening is a brief, simple procedure used to identify infants and young children who may be at risk for potential health, developmental, or social-emotional problems. It identifies children who may need a health assessment, diagnostic assessment, or educational evaluation. IEP & ITP Development in bridge Centre we work partners with educators and parents to develop an Individual Education Plan (IEP) that suits the specific child’s need. This is completed together with an Individual Therapy Plan (ITP) that can be used to promote physical and behavioral development. is committed to developing simple, straightforward, and thorough documents that jointly meet the needs of teachers, support staff, and administrators. Programs For Parents

  • Support groups
  • Positive parenting

Support groups can offer inspiration and strength. They are not meant to provide or replace treatment, but they can help complement professional treatment during recovery.

The Bridge Positive Parenting program helps give parents of children with a disability the skills to understand and manage their child’s disruptive behaviour. It is based on a model that has been demonstrated to work across cultures, socio-economic groups and with different family structures.The program is delivered in multiple formats to meet the complex needs of children and their families.
