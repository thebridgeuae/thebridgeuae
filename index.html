Applied Behavioral Analysis (ABA)

What is ABA Therapy? Is ABA Right for My Child?

Behavioural analysis is a branch of psychology that aims to comprehend, predict, and modify human behaviour. ABA is based on the fact that a client's behaviour is influenced by previous and current environmental experiences, or contingencies, as well as other genetic and/or environmental factors.

ABA focuses on treating the disorder's problems by facilitating the learning of suitable and functional behaviours rather than the dysfunctional or inappropriate actions. ABA is an evidence-based technique that has been shown to be useful not only for those with Autism but also for other diseases such as ADHD, Depression, ASD and other developmental disabilities. ABA programmes includes goals to address concerns such as:

  • Maladaptive Behaviour
  • Conversation and pre-conversational skills
  • Adaptive, daily living skills
  • Attending skills
  • Fine & gross motor skills
  • Play skills
  • Social skills
  • Communication skills
  • Imitative skills
  • Functional Academic skills

    BB Cream

    Personally, I prefer BB cream to regular foundation, as I find it to be much more natural-looking. It is a great option if you’re looking for something that has skincare benefits such as moisturizing or priming (some BB creams have primer built in).

    In addition, if you are new to the makeup world, a good BB cream is an even better place to start than foundation, as it feels lighter on the skin, is hard to overdo, and can be applied with your fingers.

    Concealer

    If you have acne, dark circles, or any kind of discoloration, concealer is a must-have.

    Concealers come in full-coverage and sheerer-coverage formulations, and which one you should choose depends on how much you’re trying to cover up.

    When choosing a concealer for acne and/or discoloration, find a shade that is as close as possible to your foundation/BB cream shade for the most natural look.
