Applied Behavioral Analysis (ABA)

What is ABA Therapy? Is ABA Right for My Child?

Behavioural analysis is a branch of psychology that aims to comprehend, predict, and modify human behaviour. ABA is based on the fact that a client's behaviour is influenced by previous and current environmental experiences, or contingencies, as well as other genetic and/or environmental factors.

ABA focuses on treating the disorder's problems by facilitating the learning of suitable and functional behaviours rather than the dysfunctional or inappropriate actions. ABA is an evidence-based technique that has been shown to be useful not only for those with Autism but also for other diseases such as ADHD, Depression, ASD and other developmental disabilities. ABA programmes includes goals to address concerns such as:

  • Maladaptive Behaviour
  • Conversation and pre-conversational skills
  • Adaptive, daily living skills
  • Attending skills
  • Fine & gross motor skills
  • Play skills
  • Social skills
  • Communication skills
  • Imitative skills
  • Functional Academic skills

Speech and Language Therapy

Speech and Language Therapy is a programme that supports children and young people (0– 19) with speech, language, and communication problems. This includes problems such as:


  • Understanding a language
  • Using a language
  • Communication and interaction
  • Dysfluency (stammering)
  • Speech sounds
  • Social skills

We also help children and young people who have eating and drinking difficulties. Some of the children we help may have exceptional needs, such as a cleft palate, hearing loss, special educational needs, or disabilities. To assist in helping the child, we collaborate closely with parents, caregivers, and other experts. Workshops, training, assessments, and group and/or individual therapy sessions are all included in this.

Early Intervention

When a child does not appear to be developing according to the regular timetable of new-born milestones, parents and family members are understandably concerned. At The Bridge, early intervention is a complete service that provides focused stimulation for both children and preschoolers with developmental delays, high-risk conditions, or disabilities.

The ultimate goal of the Early Intervention program is to assist children in learning skills related to the developmental areas that normally develop during the first three years of life such as:

  • Physical (reaching, rolling, crawling, and walking)
  • Cognitive (thinking, learning, solving problems)
  • Social (playing, feeling secure and happy)
  • Self Help (eating, dressing).

If the child has a developmental delay in one or more of these areas, the child receives individualized treatment to fulfil his or her specific requirements.

Special Educational Programs

Special education is programme targeting child who are eligible for special education because their learning and upbringing is impacted by a physical, cognitive, behavioural, or an emotional condition. This program ensures that all disabled children are able to receive normal adequate education alongside their friends and siblings.

This program provides services such as:

  • Individualized education program (IEP).
  • Occupational therapy to help with daily activities.
  • Supplemental aids and classroom aide.
  • Speech therapy to help with communicating and feeding problems.

Vocational Program

Children in our vocational programme learn critical life skills that will benefit them at home and in the community. These abilities boost self-assurance and participation in everyday activities.

The bridge’s vocational program focuses on teaching a wide range of core skills needed for employment and enhancement, such skills are:

  • Time management
  • Organisation and planning
  • Effective communication
  • Teamwork
  • Positive attitude
  • Problem solving

Family Therapy and Counseling

Family therapy is a type of counseling treatment that looks at psychological issues and how to deal with them via the interactions of family members. Families are viewed as a cohesive, interrelated system in which each family member's psychological functioning is influenced by each family member individually and collectively as an entire system.

In family therapy the focus is usually on family members' interpersonal patterns and communication. When a child has a behavior problem, for example, a family therapist is more likely to see the child's problems in the context of the family structure rather on his or her difficulties.

During family therapy, our expert team helps the family interact in a new, different way that may improve the child’s functioning system. Family therapy can help people with a variety of issues, including:

  • Anorexia nervosa
  • Abuse of drugs and alcohol
  • Obesity and overweight

Group Therapy

Group therapy is a psychotherapy treatment in which several skilled therapists work with a group of people at the same time. It’s a form of treatment that is focuses on bringing people of share similar experiences and struggles together.

Group therapy deals with a variety of conditions and mental health concerns such as social anxiety and depression. Some other examples of conditions a group may focus on include:

  • Grief
  • Chronic pain
  • Anger management
  • Post-traumatic stress disorder
  • Generalized anxiety disorder
  • Bipolar disorder
  • Panic disorder
  • Phobias
  • Attention deficit hyperactivity disorder
  • Substance use disorder
